Interior Designing

Interior designing refers to the internal environment of the place. Interior design is the art and science of enhancing the interior of a building to achieve a healthier and more aesthetically pleasing environment for the people using the space.

Landscaping

Landscaping can also be called gardening. It is the art and craft of growing plants with a goal of creating a beauty within the landscape.Landscaping refers to any activity that modifies the visible features of an area of land, including: living elements, such as flora or fauna.

Town Planning

Town planning means to plan the infrastructure of a town or urban area. It is a technical and political process concerned with the development and design of land use and the built environment, including transportation.

Elevation Design

Elevation design is the horizontal orthographic projection of a building onto a vertical plane, the vertical plane normally being parallel to one side of the building. Architects also refer to it as the north-facing wall of the building.

House planning

House planning includes planning and making the blueprints of the houses or residential area. It involves the planning of all the storeys of the houses along with the sewage system, ventilation system, smoke system, etc.

Exterior Designing

Exterior designing is to design the exterior of the building. This includes the facade, "skin", roof, and foundational elements. While exterior design can be just as creative and artistic as interior, a knowledge of structural engineering is necessary.

space and site planning

Space planning is an in-depth analysis of how physical space is used in structures. ... When the process is completed, it can result in a comprehensive space plan. This is a drawing that identifies the needs and required elements of spaces, including windows, doors, and even furniture within them.

Illustration rendering and 3D design modeling

In 3D computer graphics, 3D modeling is the process of developing a mathematical representation of any surface of an object (either inanimate or living) in three dimensions via specialized software. The product is called a 3D model. Someone who works with 3D models may be referred to as a 3D artist.
